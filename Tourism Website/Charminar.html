Charminar

The Charminar is a historic monument located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. It was built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. The structure was constructed to commemorate the founding of Hyderabad city. “Charminar” means “Four Minarets” in Urdu and Persian. It is made of granite, limestone, mortar, and pulverized marble. The monument has four grand arches facing four different directions. Each minaret is 56 meters (about 184 feet) high and has a spiral staircase. The Charminar is a blend of Indo-Islamic architecture with Persian influences. It is located in the heart of Hyderabad's bustling old city area. The monument is surrounded by famous markets like Laad Bazaar. It is one of the most recognized landmarks of Telangana. The Charminar is a symbol of Hyderabad’s culture and heritage.
