Araku

Araku Valley is a picturesque hill station located in the Eastern Ghats of Andhra Pradesh, India. It lies about 120 kilometers from Visakhapatnam and is known for its pleasant climate. The valley is famous for its lush green landscapes, coffee plantations, and tribal culture. Surrounded by thick forests, it offers breathtaking views of hills and waterfalls. The Borra Caves, with stunning stalactite and stalagmite formations, are a major attraction nearby. Araku is home to several tribal communities with unique traditions and handicrafts. The coffee grown here is considered among the finest in India. The journey to Araku by train passes through tunnels, bridges, and scenic valleys. Padmapuram Gardens and Katiki Waterfalls are popular tourist spots in the area. The climate remains cool and refreshing throughout the year. Araku Valley is often called the “Ooty of Andhra Pradesh” for its natural beauty.
